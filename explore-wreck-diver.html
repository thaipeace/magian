Wreck Diver
Bowsprit
A bowsprit is a spar that juts out in front of the ship to hold the forward triangular-shaped sails, called the foresails.
Anchor
The anchor is a very heavy piece of iron, held by a chain, that secured the ship in once place while in open water.
Yard
A yard arm is a hefty timber that is attached horizontally to a mast and to the bottom of each main sail.
Boiler
This is the boiler, which was used for heating water to create steam that powered small engines like the donkey engine.
Windlass
Another critical devide for handling the ropes! The windlass is a rotating drum that helps sailors pull ropes. It was often used to with a steam engine or a manual two-person crank.
Mast Timber
This is one of three masts of the George Marsch. the masts carried the sails and all the ringing.

All of the shipwrecks of the Great Lakes involve stories of people, ships, and tragedy. Often, all that we know of a ship lost at sea are a few details and guesses.

Explorers and archaeologists use stories and historical records to try to locate the final resting place of ships that have disappeared into the waters of the Great Lakes.
