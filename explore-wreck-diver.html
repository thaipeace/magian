George a marsh facts

The George A. Marsh was a three masted cargo schooner launched in 1882, used to carry lumber, and then coal. The ship was lost on August 8, 1917 when it sank during a summer gale.

The Marsh today is a fully intact wreck which is a tribute to what can be accomplished if divers respect our marine heritage.

Learn About This Activity

There are hundreds of shipwrecks in Eastern Lake Ontario and the St. Lawrence.

The George A. Marsh is one of the most beautifully preserved wrecks in eastern Lake Ontario and a favourite of recreational divers. Not only is it well reserved as a ship, but divers have been very careful not to remove dozens of artefacts that cover wreck site.

MISSION

Be a marine explorer and dive onto the wreck of the George A. Marsh. To visit the wreck, you will be diving 100 feet down onto the bottom of Lake Ontario where it is quite cold. You will need a good dry-suit as well as all your regular SCUBA gear.

Your mission is to locate interesting artefacts around the wreck, and record them in your journal. Of course, as an experienced diver, you know not to remove anything from the wreck. Take your time so you don’t miss anything, but watch your levels in your air tank. To get the job done, it may take you more than one dive.

Dive now!

Now that you have completed your dive check your journal to learn more about the objects that you discovered.
