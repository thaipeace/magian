What Happened To Le Griffon?

One of the oldest and greatest shipwreck mysteries of the Great Lakes is the disappearance of Le Griffon in 1679.

What Happened To Le Griffon?

Le Griffon was built on the shore of the Niagara River in 1679 by the famous explorer La Salle. It was perhaps the first full sized sailing ship on the Great Lakes.

What Happened To Le Griffon?

La Salle and his men sailed and hauled the vessel to Georgian Bay, and then to Mackinac Island and Green Bay where she was loaded with a cargo of fur.

What Happened To Le Griffon?

The ship headed back to the Niagara River, loaded with valuable furs and La Salle stayed behind to explore further. Le Griffon was never seen again.

What Happened To Le Griffon?

Underwater explorers still search for the remains of this important ship from our past.

Disappearance Of The SS Bannockburn

This is the story of the SS Bannockburn, a cargo ship built in the United Kingdom and sailed on the Great Lakes.

Disappearance Of The SS Bannockburn

In November of 1902, the SS Bannockburn was headed with a load of grain from Fort William to Kingston Ontario when a great storm blew up in the Eastern end of Lake Superior.

Disappearance Of The SS Bannockburn

She was spotted heading for the Soo Locks by the passenger steamer Huronic. After that, she was never seen or heard from again.

Disappearance Of The SS Bannockburn

A few weeks later, a lifejacket from the Bannockburn came ashore near the Grand Marias Lifesaving Station, the only debris from the Bannockburn ever found.

Disappearance Of The SS Bannockburn

No one knows exactly where the Bannockburn lies, but there have been many reports over the last century of strange ghostly sightings of her, usually just before a storm is about to begin!
